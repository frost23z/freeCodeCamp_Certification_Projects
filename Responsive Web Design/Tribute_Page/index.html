Ibn Sina

The Polymath Who Shaped Medicine and Philosophy

Here's a timeline of Ibn Sina's life:

  • 980 - Born in Afshana, a village near Bukhara in present-day Uzbekistan.
  • 986 - At age 6, he began his formal education.
  • 987 - Memorized the Quran by the age of 7.
  • 997 - Began studying philosophy and medicine under a local teacher.
  • 1000 - Became a practicing physician at the age of 20.
  • 1006 - Completed his education in Gurganj (present-day Urgench, Uzbekistan).
  • 1010 - Began to study medicine in earnest and quickly distinguished himself as a brilliant student.
  • 1012 - Wrote "The Book of Healing," a comprehensive scientific and philosophical encyclopedia.
  • 1020 - Appointed as the physician to the emir of Bukhara.
  • 1025 - Wrote the Canon of Medicine, an encyclopedia of medicine that became a standard medical text at many medieval universities and remained in use as late as 1650.
  • 1030 - His works started influencing medical education in Europe.
  • 1037 - Died in Hamadan, Persia.

Ibn Sina's legacy is a testament to the enduring power of knowledge and learning. His life and works continue to inspire scholars and students across the world. His contributions remind us of the importance of intellectual curiosity and the pursuit of understanding.
